If you're interested in what's involved in planning a mass cytometry project or are planning a project for yourself, then you've come to the right place. Here, we'll spell out the may things you need to think about when planning your project. A little planning will let you reap the highest rewards.

A mass cytometry project workflow consists of:
  1. Define your scientific question
  2. Panel Design
  3. Reagent Acquisition
  4. Reagent Validation
  5. Sample Staining (and Optimization)
  6. Acquisition
  7. Data Analysis

Your Scientific Question


It's is very important that you have a clear scientific question in mind. This question defines the markers we need to analyze, which defines your panel. 40-50 parameters are a lot in cytometry terms but relatively small compared to other -omics platforms. Without a clear question in mind it's very easy to acquire precious samples and realize we haven't measured everything we needed. We need to use each and every one of them to drill down on your specific question.
